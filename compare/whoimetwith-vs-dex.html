The Core Difference

Who I Met and Dex are solving the same problem — you meet too many people to track manually — but their philosophies are completely different.

Who I Met starts with your calendar. Your meetings are the source of truth. Every person you've ever had a calendar event with becomes a contact automatically, complete with full meeting history. No manual entry, no imports, no setup. Connect your Google Calendar and your entire professional network appears.

Dex is a more traditional CRM experience. It pulls from email and LinkedIn, and gives you a structured place to write relationship notes, set reminders, and track how you know people. It's proactive about nudging you to reach out. But it requires more manual input to get value.

💡 The key question: Do your meaningful professional relationships happen through scheduled calendar meetings? If yes, Who I Met will feel like magic. If your most important relationships happen mostly over text/email with no calendar touchpoints, Dex may be a better fit.

Who I Met: Strengths

Zero-Entry Contact Discovery

The biggest differentiator. Who I Met reads your calendar history and instantly surfaces everyone you've met — going back up to 3 years. Most users see hundreds of contacts they'd completely forgotten about within minutes of connecting their calendar. Dex requires you to add contacts manually or import them.

Contact Enrichment

Turn any email address into a full professional profile: LinkedIn URL, current job title, company, and bio. Dex doesn't offer this — you'd need to manually add context or connect other tools.

Built-In Mail Merge

Email your contacts directly from Who I Met with personalized merge variables (name, company, title). Great for founder updates, launch announcements, or sales follow-ups. Dex doesn't have bulk email functionality.

Significantly Lower Price

Who I Met Pro is $34.99/year (under $3/month). Dex runs $12/month ($144/year) — over 4x the cost. For professionals who just want a smart contact database without full CRM overhead, the value difference is significant.

Dex: Strengths

Proactive Relationship Nudges

Dex will remind you when you haven't been in touch with someone for a while. This "keep in touch" feature is built around helping you nurture dormant relationships before they go cold. Who I Met shows you the data (last met, meeting frequency) but doesn't send reminders.

Rich Manual Notes

Dex is built for people who like to write detailed relationship notes — how you know someone, what you talked about, personal details. It's more CRM-like. Who I Met uses calendar event titles as context, which works well but isn't as structured for note-heavy users.

Native Mobile App

Dex has polished iOS and Android apps. Who I Met is a web app that works on mobile browsers but doesn't have a native app yet.

Which Should You Choose?

✓ Choose Who I Met if you...

  • Schedule most meetings via Google Calendar
  • Want zero manual data entry
  • Need contact enrichment (LinkedIn data)
  • Want to send personalized bulk emails to your network
  • Value simplicity and lower cost
  • Are a founder, VC, recruiter, or sales pro
  • Want to rediscover contacts from years of past meetings

→ Choose Dex if you...

  • Prefer a mobile-first CRM experience
  • Want proactive reminders to reach out
  • Like writing detailed personal relationship notes
  • Your key relationships don't involve many calendar meetings
  • Want a more traditional CRM structure

Bottom Line

If you live in your calendar, Who I Met will feel like the product you always wanted but didn't know existed. It turns years of meeting data into an organized, searchable, actionable network — instantly, with no work required.

Dex is a solid choice if you're more of a note-taker who wants CRM-style relationship tracking with mobile access and reminder nudges. But at 4x the price and with no automatic contact extraction, it requires a lot more effort to get the same value.
