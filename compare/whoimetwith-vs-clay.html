Overview: Different Philosophies

WhoIMetWith: Calendar-First Simplicity

WhoIMetWith takes a focused approach: your calendar already contains a record of everyone you've met professionally. Rather than trying to sync everything, it extracts meeting attendees from Google Calendar and builds a clean contact database from that single source.

This approach trades comprehensiveness for simplicity. You won't capture email-only contacts or LinkedIn connections you've never met, but you get zero-friction tracking of everyone you've actually had a meeting with.

Clay: Multi-Source Aggregation

Clay aims to be a comprehensive relationship hub by syncing contacts from email, calendar, LinkedIn, Twitter, and messaging apps. Its Nexus AI lets you search your network with natural language queries like "who do I know at Google?"

This approach offers more complete coverage but requires more setup and can feel overwhelming if you have large networks across multiple platforms.

Feature Deep Dive

Data Sources & Sync

WhoIMetWith connects to Google Calendar via OAuth and extracts attendees from your meetings. You configure a date range (last month, year, or all time) and contacts populate automatically. That's it—simple, focused, and reliable.

Clay offers broader sync options: Gmail, Google Calendar, Outlook, LinkedIn, Twitter, Facebook, WhatsApp, and iMessage. More sources means more comprehensive data, but also more potential sync issues and a messier initial import.

Contact Enrichment

WhoIMetWith uses a credit-based system for enrichment. You buy credit packs ($15 for 50, $35 for 150, or $99 for 500) and use them to enrich individual contacts with LinkedIn profiles, company info, and job titles. You only pay for what you use.

Clay includes enrichment as part of the Pro plan. Contacts are automatically enriched with available data. This is more convenient but means you pay monthly whether you need enrichment or not.

Meeting History & Context

WhoIMetWith excels here since meetings are its core focus. For every contact, you see first met date, last met date, total meeting count, and a complete list of every calendar event with that person. It's immediately clear how deep the relationship is.

Clay also tracks meeting history but blends it with email and messaging interactions. The timeline view is comprehensive but can be noisier if you have lots of email threads.

Organization & Lists

Both tools offer custom lists for organizing contacts. WhoIMetWith supports bulk adding/removing from lists and CSV/PDF export of any list. Clay has similar functionality plus tags and smart groups based on attributes.

Mobile & Desktop Access

WhoIMetWith is currently web-only. Clay offers native apps for iOS, Android, Mac, and Windows. If mobile access is critical, Clay has the advantage.

AI Features

Clay offers Nexus AI, which lets you query your network in natural language. Ask "who do I know in fintech?" and get relevant results. WhoIMetWith doesn't have AI features—it focuses on straightforward search and filter instead.

Pricing Comparison

WhoIMetWith Pricing

  • Free: Unlimited contacts, calendar sync, lists, export—forever free
  • Enrichment credits: $15/50 credits, $35/150 credits, $99/500 credits
  • No monthly subscription required

Clay Pricing

  • Personal: Free, up to 1,000 contacts
  • Pro: $10/month (annual) or $20/month (monthly)
  • Team: $40/user/month for team features

For users who only occasionally need enrichment, WhoIMetWith's credit model can be significantly cheaper than Clay's subscription. For heavy enrichment users who want all-in-one features, Clay Pro might be better value.

The Verdict: Which Should You Choose?

Choose WhoIMetWith if:

  • Most of your networking happens in meetings
  • You want zero-setup automatic tracking
  • You don't need email or social sync
  • You prefer pay-as-you-go pricing
  • You value simplicity over features

Choose Clay if:

  • You network heavily across email and social
  • You want AI-powered network search
  • Mobile app access is important
  • You want automatic enrichment included
  • You prefer a comprehensive all-in-one tool

Can You Use Both?

Yes! Some users run WhoIMetWith for meeting tracking and Clay for broader relationship context. Both tools export to CSV, so you can also start with one and migrate if your needs change. The best tool is the one you'll actually use consistently.
