The Launch List Problem

You're launching something new. You need people to try it, share it, upvote it, provide feedback. Where do you start?

Most founders default to cold outreach—Twitter DMs, LinkedIn spam, email blasts to purchased lists. Response rates are abysmal. You're competing with every other launch for attention from people who don't know you.

But here's the thing: over the past year, you've probably met hundreds of people through calls, meetings, and coffees. These people already know who you are. They've invested time talking with you. They're infinitely more likely to support your launch than a stranger.

The problem? Those contacts are scattered across months of calendar entries. Finding them feels like archaeological excavation.

Cold List vs. Warm List: The Numbers Don't Lie

Cold Outreach

  • 2-5% response rate
  • Feels spammy to recipients
  • Takes huge volume to get results
  • Damages your reputation
  • Low-quality engagement

Warm Outreach (People You've Met)

  • 30-50%+ response rate
  • Personal and welcomed
  • Smaller list, bigger impact
  • Strengthens relationships
  • Genuine support and feedback

A warm list of 200 people who actually know you will outperform a cold list of 2,000 strangers every time.

What Makes a Good Launch List Contact?

High Priority

  • Potential users: People who might actually use your product
  • Industry peers: Others in your space who understand the problem
  • Investors you've talked to: They want to see you succeed
  • Journalists/creators you've met: They might cover your launch
  • Frequent contacts: People you've met multiple times (stronger relationship)

Medium Priority

  • Adjacent industry contacts: May share with their network
  • One-time meetings with good rapport: Worth a personal note
  • Former colleagues: Generally supportive

Don't Add Everyone

Resist the temptation to blast your entire contact list. People who have no reason to care about your product won't support your launch—and mass outreach damages relationships. Quality over quantity.

Launch List Templates

Product Hunt Launch

Focus on: tech-savvy contacts, other founders, people active on Product Hunt, anyone who's launched before and understands the game. Timing matters—reach out 1-2 days before with a specific ask (upvote, comment, share).

Beta User Recruitment

Focus on: people who fit your ICP, those who've expressed related pain points in past conversations, existing users of similar tools. Ask for feedback, not just sign-ups.

Fundraising Announcement

Focus on: investors you've talked to (even passes), founder friends, potential customers who care about your trajectory, journalists covering your space. A funding announcement is news—share it with people who would actually want to know.

Content/Newsletter Launch

Focus on: people interested in your topic, others who create similar content (potential collaborators), industry contacts who would share good content. Ask them to subscribe and share if they find it valuable.

The Personal Touch Makes the Difference

The magic of a warm launch list isn't just higher response rates—it's the quality of those responses. When you reach out to someone you've actually met, you can personalize:

  • "Remember when we talked about X problem? I built a solution."
  • "You mentioned struggling with Y—this might help."
  • "Since our conversation at Z conference, I've been working on this."
