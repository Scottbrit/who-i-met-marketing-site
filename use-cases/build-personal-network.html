The Networking Paradox

Everyone tells you to "build your network." Attend events. Take coffee meetings. Connect on LinkedIn. But then what?

You meet interesting people. You have great conversations. You swap contact info and promise to "stay in touch." Three months later, you can't remember their name, what you talked about, or why you were excited to meet them.

This is the networking paradox: the act of meeting people is easy, but the act of maintaining relationships is hard. Without a system, connections fade. Opportunities disappear. Your "network" becomes a graveyard of forgotten conversations.

What Makes a Network Valuable?

A powerful network isn't about quantity—it's about maintained relationships. Consider:

  • 100 contacts you can't remember = essentially worthless
  • 20 contacts you have context on = actually useful
  • 10 contacts you regularly nurture = genuinely powerful

The goal isn't to meet more people. It's to remember and nurture the people you've already met. That's where WhoIMetWith comes in.

The Compound Effect of Maintained Relationships

Relationships compound like interest. A contact you met once and forgot about has zero value. A contact you've maintained over years becomes someone who:

  • Thinks of you when opportunities arise
  • Introduces you to their network
  • Provides advice and support
  • Champions your work to others

But you can't maintain what you can't remember. WhoIMetWith gives you the foundation: a complete record of everyone you've met, so you can be intentional about who you nurture.

Practical Network-Building Workflows

Weekly Network Review (15 mins)

Once a week, open WhoIMetWith and review your contacts. Who did you meet recently? Anyone you should follow up with? Any relationships going cold that you want to revive? A quick scan keeps your network top of mind.

Pre-Meeting Prep

Before any meeting, check your history with that person. When did you first meet? How many times have you talked? What meetings have you had together? Walk in with full context instead of scrambling to remember.

Quarterly Reconnection

Each quarter, filter your contacts by "last met" to find people you haven't talked to in 3+ months. Create a "Reconnect" list. Work through it with personal outreach: "It's been a while since we connected—how have things been?"

Network Mapping

Use lists to map your network by category: mentors, peers, potential clients, industry experts, personal friends. Seeing your network structured helps you identify gaps and opportunities.
